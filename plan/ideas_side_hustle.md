1. No-Code AI Chatbot Development

    First 3 Concrete Steps to Get Started:

        Create a free account on Typebot or Voiceflow. Build a simple, working mock-up chatbot designed for a specific local niche (e.g., a bot that schedules appointments for a beauty salon).

        Connect your template bot to a free OpenAI API key and test it to ensure it answers basic questions naturally.

        Record a 60-second video of your screen showing the bot in action, specifically highlighting how it captures a name, phone number, and preferred time slot seamlessly.

    How to Get the First Paying Customer:
    Identify 10 local businesses in Fortaleza that have a WhatsApp link on their Instagram but take hours to reply. Send them the 60-second video via Instagram DM. Offer to install the system for free for 7 days, charging only a modest setup fee after they see the automated leads rolling in.

    Common Mistake to Avoid:
    Trying to build a complex, all-knowing AI. Keep the bot's scope small. Limit it to answering 10 specific FAQs and capturing user contact info before routing it to a human. Over-complicating it leads to hallucinated answers and frustrated clients.

1: AI + WhatsApp Automation for Local SMBs

First 3 concrete steps:


Pick one vertical you can talk to confidently (e.g., dental clinics, real estate agents, or restaurants) and map their three most repetitive WhatsApp tasks — appointment confirmations, FAQ responses, and lead follow-up are nearly universal.
Build one free demo flow in n8n or Make connected to a test WhatsApp Business number, simulating exactly what that vertical needs. Record a 90-second screen video showing it working.
Set a simple two-tier offer: a one-time setup fee (anchor it around R$1,200–1,800) plus a modest monthly maintenance retainer (R$350–600).


How to get the first paying customer:
Don't cold-pitch strangers — go to a business you already have a relationship with (your dentist, a friend's salon, a local restaurant you frequent) and offer to build it for a discounted "founding client" rate in exchange for a testimonial and the right to use their results as a case study. That case study becomes your sales asset for the next ten outreach messages to similar businesses in Fortaleza.

One common mistake to avoid:
Selling "AI automation" as the pitch instead of the specific time/money saved. Local business owners don't buy technology — they buy "you stop losing customers who message at 10pm and never hear back." Always lead with the business outcome, not the tool stack.

1) WhatsApp AI setup for small businesses

This is the strongest option because WhatsApp is already a core sales channel in Brazil, and automation can immediately improve response speed, lead capture, and conversion. It also has the best path to recurring revenue because you can charge for setup plus monthly maintenance and optimization.

First 3 steps:

    Pick one niche, such as salons, clinics, or restaurants, and create one demo flow.

    Build a simple example in WhatsApp Business plus a no-code automation tool.

    Create a one-page offer: “24/7 WhatsApp assistant that answers FAQs and captures leads.”

How to get the first paying customer:

    Message 30 local businesses with slow WhatsApp response times and offer a free teardown of their current process.

    Show a short before/after demo and pitch a low-risk setup fee.

    Close with a simple promise: fewer missed leads and faster response times.

Common mistake to avoid:

    Building a complex bot before validating demand; start with 5–10 high-frequency questions and one clear handoff to a human.
